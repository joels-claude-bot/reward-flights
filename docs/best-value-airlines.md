# Best-value airlines for Amex UK points

There is no single "best" programme — value depends on *where* you're flying and *what
cabin* (class of travel). But some American Express (Amex) UK partners punch above their
weight from the UK. This page ranks them by how reliably an **MR (Membership Rewards)
point** turns into outsized value.

:::tip 30-second version
For most people the order is: **Virgin Atlantic** and **Avios** (British Airways / Iberia /
Qatar) first — both 1:1 and instant; **Flying Blue** and **Qantas** next; **Singapore** and
**Emirates** only for a specific prize (their ratios are worse than 1:1).
:::

:::warning These rankings are heuristics — validate each booking
The tiers below are rules of thumb from general reward-travel experience, not a guarantee for
your trip. A "Tier 3" programme on the right seat beats a "Tier 1" on the wrong one — so always
confirm the specific redemption in the [calculator](/expected-value) before transferring.
:::

## Tier 1 — start here

### Virgin Atlantic Flying Club (Virgin Points) — 1:1, instant
- **Why:** Instant transfers, and access to partner award charts that hide real bargains —
  notably **ANA (All Nippon Airways) first/business to Tokyo** and **Delta One (Delta's
  business class) to the US**. The Virgin chart itself is reasonable on its own aircraft.
- **Watch:** Surcharges on Virgin's own flights can be high; partner redemptions often
  cheaper on fees.

### British Airways Club / the Avios cluster — 1:1, instant
- **Why:** The most *flexible* currency. One Avios balance works across British Airways,
  Iberia, Qatar and Aer Lingus. **Short-haul reward flights** (Avios + small cash via
  "Reward Flight Saver") are the bread-and-butter sweet spot. **Qatar Qsuite** business
  class is a standout long-haul redemption, bookable with Avios.
- **Watch:** British Airways long-haul carries large surcharges; **Iberia and Qatar
  often charge far lower fees** for the same Avios — book the same seat through them.

## Tier 2 — situationally excellent

### Flying Blue (Air France / KLM) — 1:1, ~4 working days
- **Why:** Monthly **"Promo Rewards"** discount 25–50% off specific routes; dynamic
  pricing occasionally throws up cheap premium seats to Europe and Africa.
- **Watch:** Dynamic pricing means no fixed chart — value swings a lot.

### Qantas Frequent Flyer — 1:1, ~3 business days
- **Why:** Strong **oneworld partner** award chart (including good value on some
  British Airways and Qatar routes), and the obvious pick for **Australia**.

## Tier 3 — only for a specific prize

### Singapore KrisFlyer — 3:2, up to 15 working days
- **Why:** **Singapore Suites / business class** is a bucket-list redemption you often
  *can't* book through anyone else.
- **Watch:** Sub-1:1 ratio **and** a punishing 15-day transfer time — only transfer once
  you can see the seat and can wait.

### Emirates Skywards — 2:1, ~2 working days
- **Why:** Emirates first class. That's largely it.
- **Watch:** 2:1 ratio plus high surcharges — the maths rarely works outside premium cabins.

## Quick ranking

| Tier | Programme | Ratio | Speed | Best for |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Virgin Atlantic | 1:1 | Instant | Partner premium cabins (ANA, Delta) |
| 1 | Avios cluster (BA/Iberia/Qatar) | 1:1 | Instant–48h | Short-haul + Qatar Qsuite |
| 2 | Flying Blue | 1:1 | ~4 days | Promo Rewards, Europe/Africa |
| 2 | Qantas | 1:1 | ~3 days | oneworld + Australia |
| 3 | Singapore | 3:2 | ~15 days | Singapore Suites |
| 3 | Emirates | 2:1 | ~2 days | Emirates first class |

:::tip One rule beats all rankings
Whichever programme you're eyeing, **run the specific booking through the
[calculator](/expected-value)**. A Tier 3 programme on the right seat beats a Tier 1
programme on the wrong one.
:::

> Sub-1:1 ratios (Emirates, Singapore) and surcharge levels are the two things that most
> often quietly destroy value — always check both before transferring.
