# Finding award seats

The hard part of reward flights isn't the points — it's finding a **bookable award seat**
(a seat the airline has released for points). Each airline's own site shows only *its own*
seats, so the practical question is: **for the airline I want, which search tool covers it?**

## Which tool covers which airline

:::tip 30-second version
Start with **[seats.aero](https://seats.aero/)** — its free tier has the widest coverage (8 of
11 partners). For **British Airways, Iberia or Cathay**, use **[SeatSpy](https://www.seatspy.com/)**.
**[PointsYeah](https://www.pointsyeah.com/)** (free) is a great alternative for Virgin Atlantic,
Flying Blue, Qatar, Qantas and Delta. **[AwardTool](https://www.awardtool.com/)** casts the

## What the matrix tells you

- **No single tool covers everything.** British Airways, Iberia and Cathay are the clearest gap —
  only **SeatSpy** (and AwardTool) track them; seats.aero and PointsYeah don't.
- **seats.aero has the widest *free* coverage** — 8 of 11 partners, including Emirates, Singapore
  and SAS, which PointsYeah doesn't track.
- **PointsYeah overlaps on the popular five** (Virgin Atlantic, Flying Blue, Qatar, Qantas, Delta)
  — so for those you have a free choice of two tools.
- **A `?` means unconfirmed**, not "no" — coverage changes, so check the tool's own
  supported-programmes page (linked in the matrix).

## seats.aero — status by Amex UK partner

seats.aero tracks 8 of the 11 Amex UK partners (not British Airways, Iberia or Cathay). Each
programme has a live status — an "Outage" or "Degraded" is usually temporary, so re-check later
rather than writing the tool off.

## SeatSpy — coverage by Amex UK partner

SeatSpy is the UK specialist: it's the go-to for **British Airways and Iberia Avios**, and also
covers Cathay, Flying Blue and Virgin Atlantic — the partners the others miss.

<SeatSpyTable scope="amex" />

## PointsYeah — coverage by Amex UK partner

PointsYeah is free for unlimited basic searches and reads your Amex Membership Rewards balance
directly. It covers the **popular five** — Virgin Atlantic, Flying Blue, Qatar, Qantas and Delta —
but not the Avios specialists (BA, Iberia) or Emirates / Singapore / SAS.

<PointsYeahTable scope="amex" />

These tools also cover many airlines that **aren't** Amex UK transfer partners (United,
American, Air Canada and more) — useful if you also hold other points currencies. See the
[Appendix: airlines beyond Amex UK](/appendix).

## The efficient workflow

1. **Discover space** with a free-tier tool (seats.aero / PointsYeah) — *where* do seats exist?
2. **Set an alert** if nothing fits your dates yet (seats.aero Pro, AwardTool, PointsYeah all do this).
3. **Confirm on the airline's own site** — the only authoritative source.
4. **Transfer points and book** — see [When to book](/when-to-book).

:::tip Alerts are the real quality-of-life win
The biggest time-saver is *not searching repeatedly*. Set an alert for your route, cabin and
date range, then forget it until it pings. This one change makes reward-flight booking pleasant
rather than a chore.
:::

## Searching without burning hours

- **Flex dates first, route second.** One day either side often halves the cost.
- **Two seats are much harder than one.** Travelling as a pair? Prioritise the schedule-open day
  and the last-minute window, where multi-seat space is likeliest (see [When to book](/when-to-book)).
- **Mixed cabins are fine.** Business out, economy back can still be a brilliant booking.

## Other tools worth knowing

Beyond the four above: **[point.me](https://www.point.me/)** (beginner-friendly "tell me what to
book") and **[ExpertFlyer](https://www.expertflyer.com/)** (power-user seat-map data and alerts).
